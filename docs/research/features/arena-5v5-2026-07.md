# Arenas 5v5 Research Record

Checked: 2026-07-13

## Historical Rules

The original Arenas mode used two teams of three. A round ended when one team
was eliminated. Players did not respawn during a round, although the original
game still allowed a downed teammate to be revived.

Each round began behind spawn barriers with a 30-second buy period. The map
contained extended supply bins, material canisters, one care package, and a
closing Ring. A team needed at least three round wins and a two-round lead to
win the match. Round nine was sudden death.

The original EA article linked by historical references now returns a 404.

## Lapex Adaptation

Lapex keeps the round structure, prep barriers, elimination rule, supply
containers, care-package contest, Ring pressure, win-by-two scoring, and
round-nine sudden death. It deliberately changes these parts:

| Rule | Lapex decision | Reason |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Team size | Five versus five | Requested playtest format. |
| Map | Compact Foundry map | The 640-by-640 Kings Canyon adaptation is too broad for ten-player fights. |
| Buy menu | Chosen round loadout plus map loot | A complete attachment, consumable, material, and ability economy does not exist yet. |
| Downed state | Immediate elimination | Lapex has no safe downed-body and revive subsystem yet. |
| Missing players | Native combat bots fill empty slots | One player can run a meaningful local playtest without Citizens. |
| Loot bins | Script-owned claims, not chest inventories | Prevents hoppers, inventory duplication, and stale loot between rounds. |

This is an Arenas-inspired adaptation, not a one-to-one restoration. The mode
must not be described as exact until the original attachment, material, and
consumable purchase economy, downing, revives, and live ten-client tests exist.

## Required Behavior

1. A match has exactly five valid combat slots on each team after bots fill
   empty human slots.
2. Prep lasts 30 seconds and blocks weapons, abilities, and leaving the spawn
   room.
3. Eliminated actors stay out until the next round.
4. Supply containers reset once at the start of every round and cannot grant
   duplicate claims.
5. The care container offers stronger weapons as rounds advance.
6. The Ring starts after the opening fight has developed and shrinks toward
   the center objective.
7. Three wins are enough only with a two-round lead. Round nine always decides
   the match.
8. Every delayed queue checks the exact match session before changing state.
9. Leaving, stopping, reloading scripts, or restarting removes bots and
   restores each human player's saved state.

## Test Cases

- Validate five unique safe spawn points for both teams.
- Validate `3-0`, `3-1`, `3-2`, `4-2`, `4-3`, and round-nine score paths.
- Attempt to shoot, use an ability, or cross the barrier during prep.
- Claim each loot container twice and from both teams in one round.
- Kill a human and a bot, then confirm neither returns before the next round.
- Test human-to-bot, bot-to-human, and bot-to-bot friendly-fire rejection.
- Put a Dome, phase effect, Crypto body, and destructible device in a bot shot.
- Stop and reload during every phase, then check for stale flags or entities.
- Run ten bots for five minutes while watching server tick time.

## Implementation Verification

Local Paper verification on 2026-07-13 covered all registry checks, ten native
bots spawning and firing, 130 authored chunks remaining active, cleanup after
script reload and admin stop, and one complete all-bot round. That round
advanced to round two with Blue leading `1-0`; Paper reported 20 TPS during
combat. The final reload-sound fix then ran through repeated magazines without
console errors.

This does not replace a ten-client test. Human loot clicks, simultaneous claim
contention, player state restoration, and long multiplayer soak testing remain
manual checklist items.
